About the Artist

Casey Hilton

My work explores the spaces between memory and identity, examining how personal history shapes the way we understand ourselves and others. Through painting and mixed-media practices, I investigate themes of family, grief, mental health, relationships, and the stories we inherit — whether willingly or not.

I am drawn to moments of contradiction: strength and vulnerability, permanence and impermanence, connection and isolation. Using layered imagery, symbolism, and narrative elements, I create work that reflects the complexity of lived experience. Personal memories often serve as a starting point, but the goal is to create space for viewers to find echoes of their own experiences within the work.

My practice is rooted in observation, introspection, and experimentation. Influenced by both traditional fine art processes and contemporary visual culture, I move between painting, drawing, sculpture, printmaking, and digital media depending on what best serves the concept. Rather than offering definitive answers, I aim to ask questions about who we are, how we change, and what remains after time, loss, and growth reshape our understanding of ourselves.

Ultimately, my work is an invitation to reflect on the fragile, often messy nature of being human — and to find beauty within that complexity.

Practice

Ways of Working

Painting

Oil and mixed media on canvas and board, layered over time to build depth and texture.

Drawing

Observational and intuitive drawing as a daily practice of seeing and thinking through form.

Sculpture

Wood-based sculptural work exploring organic form, material honesty, and tactile presence.

Printmaking

Screen printing as a way of distilling imagery into bold, repeatable gestures and layers.
